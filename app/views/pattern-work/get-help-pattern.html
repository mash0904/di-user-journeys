When to use this pattern

Use this in the footer of every page of your service.

Placing this consistently across all pages helps users learn where to get help from anywhere within your service.

Consistent placement of help also aligns with WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 3.2.6 Consistent Help (Level A).

How it works

Users are more likely to seek help when they have tried to solve a problem but are still having trouble.

Position this within the footer block of your page, right before the main GOV.UK footer.

This allows users to read the page guidance and try to fix problems on their own before seeking further help.

Sharing contact details outside your service

Use a link that opens in a new tab when you have to direct users away from your service to access contact or help information on another page.

Make it as easy as possible for users to navigate back to your service, as research has shown some users can experience difficulty switching between tabs or knowing how to return to a service page.

Sharing contact details within your service

Use a details component when you want to share contact details within your service.

Consider how you might also link to web guidance alongside contact details.

Research on this pattern

This pattern was developed by the Central UCD team. The team tested this pattern as part of the broader GOV.UK One Login contact centre work. They ran 2 rounds of research with 12 participants in total.

The examples and guidance here are based on patterns used by HM Courts & Tribunals Service (HMCTS), Ministry of Justice (MoJ) and the GOV.UK Design System.
